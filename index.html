Upgrade Computer

CPU Upgrades

The CPU (Central Processing Unit) is the brains of your computer and carries out all the calculations.







RAM Upgrades

The RAM (Random Access Memory) is where your computer stores all the data being used at the moment. When the computer is switched off, the data is lost.

HDD Upgrades

The HDD (Hard Disk Drive) stores all your files and data. The data stored here is kept until manually deleted.

Video Card Upgrades

The Video Card connects the computer to its display and controls the images displayed on the screen.

Screen Size Upgrades

The screen size determines how much room you have on your desktop to work with.

Cooling Fan RPM Upgrades

The cooling fan's RPM (Rotations per Minute) determines how quickly and effective the computer can cool the computer when it heats up.

Cooling Fan Noise Level Upgrades

The cooling fan's noise level states how loud the fan is when it is spinning at its fastest. Ideally this should be very quiet.

Extra Features Upgrades

Computers can come with many additional feaures which are not necessary for it to function
